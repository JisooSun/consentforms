Vanderbilt University — Informed Sheet

Title:                            Mechanisms and abilities in high-level perception

Principal Investigator:    Dr. Isabel Gauthier — Psychology Department

This research study, conducted at Vanderbilt University by Prof. Isabel Gauthier, is designed to help our understanding of how people perceive and recognize objects. You may first be asked to provide information about yourself (e.g., sex, age, race, native language, highest level of education completed)

The study can include the following tasks (but possibility not all of them):

    Surveys including questions about your personality, your attitudes and your experience in a certain domain.
    Perceptual tasks including visual and/or auditory tasks asking you to make simple decisions about images (e.g., letters, words, faces, familiar or unfamiliar objects) or sounds (e.g., bird songs).
    Cognitive tasks with images, words, letters or numbers that test your knowledge of a domain or your abilities to solve different kinds of puzzles.

Your participation is voluntary and you may discontinue participation at any time without penalty. Participation involves no more than minimal risk. You are under no obligation to complete any other study.

Confidentiality: All reasonable efforts will be made to keep the personal information in your research record confidential but absolute confidentiality cannot be guaranteed. Your data will be identified by number only. To ensure that the same participant is not tested more than once, each participant’s name and contact information will be recorded. A reference table that associates each person’s name with his or her data will be maintained. Only the Research Personnel will have access to this table, which will be kept in a locked filing cabinet in the laboratory. Disclosures that you agree to in this document are not protected. This includes putting research data in the medical record or sharing research data for this study or future r esearch. Disclosures that you make yourself are also not protected. Your data, without any personal information attached, may be shared in public data repository to provide the research community with open access to scientific data.

Compensation: If you are participating in this study for course credit, you will be compensated at the rate of 1 unit of credit for each 1/2 hour of participation. Remember that you have the option not to participate in this study and to write short papers for course credit instead, as described by the instructor of your class. If you are participating for pay, you will be compensated at a rate of $12 per hour.

Questions or Concerns: You can ask questions about this research study now or any time during the study by e - mailing Isabel Gauthier at Isabel.gauthier@vanderbilt.edu. If you have questions about your rights as a research participant or feel that you have not been treated fairly, please contact the Vanderbilt University Human Research Protection Program Office at 615-322-2918, or toll-free 866-224-8273
